Udacity

Udacity is an online education platform that offers courses, primarily focused on technology and career-oriented skills. It was founded in 2011 by Sebastian Thrun, David Stavens, and Mike Sokolsky. Initially, it emerged from a Stanford University experiment, where Thrun and fellow professor Peter Norvig offered their "Introduction to Artificial Intelligence" course online for free, attracting thousands of students worldwide.

Key Features:

  • Nanodegrees: These are career-focused courses that combine video lessons, projects, and mentorship to help students gain practical, job-ready skills.
  • Industry Collaboration: Udacity collaborates with tech giants like Google, IBM, Microsoft, Amazon, and Nvidia to co-create courses.
  • Project-Based Learning: Instead of exams, Udacity emphasizes hands-on learning through projects that simulate real-world problems.
